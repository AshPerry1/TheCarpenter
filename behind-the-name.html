Behind the Name

Why we're called The Carpenter—and why it matters for your work

Jesus: The Carpenter

"Is not this the carpenter, the son of Mary...?"

— Mark 6:3 (ESV)

Before Jesus was a rabbi, a miracle worker, or the Savior of the world, He was known in His hometown as "the carpenter."

For roughly 18 years—from His youth until He began His public ministry at age 30—Jesus worked with His hands. He crafted wood, built furniture, repaired tools, and served customers in first-century Nazareth. He experienced the dignity and the toil of everyday work.

This matters profoundly. The eternal Son of God—through whom all things were created—chose to spend most of His earthly life doing ordinary, physical labor. Not in the temple. Not preaching. Just... working.

He Became Poor for Our Sake

"For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor, so that you by his poverty might become rich."

— 2 Corinthians 8:9 (ESV)

Jesus didn't come as a wealthy merchant or powerful ruler. He came as a working-class tradesman in a small town. He knew tight budgets, long hours, demanding customers, and physical exhaustion.

He understands your Monday morning. Your difficult boss. Your tight deadlines. Your mundane tasks. Your frustration when work feels meaningless.

By becoming a carpenter, Jesus dignified all honest work. He showed us that faithfulness in ordinary labor—done for God's glory and our neighbor's good—matters deeply to Him.

Why This Name Matters for You

We call this site The Carpenter because Jesus—the Carpenter of Nazareth—dignified all honest work. His life shows us that work isn't just about earning a living; it has deeper meaning and purpose.

  • Because He was a carpenter, we know that all honest work—from construction to coding—has dignity and worth.
  • Because He worked with His hands for nearly two decades, we know that years of faithful, ordinary labor matter.
  • Because He served customers and community before His ministry began, we know quiet faithfulness is never wasted.
  • Because He became poor and experienced workplace challenges, we know He understands your Monday morning struggles.
  • Because He changed everything, we can find true purpose in our daily work—whatever it is.

Your Invitation

Whether you already follow Jesus or are just beginning to explore who He is, you're invited to discover that your work—whatever it is—can have meaning, purpose, and eternal significance.

When we work with integrity, excellence, and love, we follow in the footsteps of Jesus the Carpenter—the One who showed us that all honest work has profound worth.

"Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the inheritance as your reward. You are serving the Lord Christ."

— Colossians 3:23-24 (ESV)
