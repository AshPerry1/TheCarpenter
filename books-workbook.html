How to Use This Guide

This workbook helps you internalize and act on what you read. For each chapter, you'll find reflection questions, action steps, and memory verses.

Encourage reading with prayer, reflection, and action. Growth comes by obedience. It won't be perfect, but God honors faithful steps.

Every Good Endeavor — Timothy Keller

Chapter 1: The Design of Work

Reflection Questions
  • How does seeing work as God's original design (not a result of the Fall) change your perspective?
  • In what ways does your job allow you to "cultivate and keep" something in God's world?
  • Where do you see God's image reflected in your work or your coworkers?
Action Step: Pause tomorrow and thank God for one specific way your job mirrors creation.

Chapter 2: The Dignity of Work

Reflection Questions
  • What tasks in your job do you tend to dismiss as "just busy work"?
  • How might those tasks actually serve your neighbor or contribute to the common good?
  • What would change if you saw every task as service to God?
Action Step: Identify one mundane task and reframe it as service to neighbor—do it with renewed intention.

Chapter 5: Work Becomes Fruitless

Reflection Questions
  • What frustrations or disappointments do you experience in your work?
  • How has sin affected your industry or workplace?
  • Where might God be redeeming even the brokenness you see?
Action Step: Write down one work frustration. Pray and ask God where there is redeeming purpose even in trial.

Chapter 11: A New Compass for Work

Reflection Questions
  • What motivates your work most: approval, money, purpose, or God's glory?
  • How can you pursue excellence without making work an idol?
  • What would "working as for the Lord" look like in your specific role?
Action Step: Pick one "extra mile" move tomorrow (kindness, quality, clarity) and do it for God, not human approval.

Memory Verses

"Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men." — Colossians 3:23 (ESV)

"Your labor in the Lord is not in vain." — 1 Corinthians 15:58 (ESV)

The Sacredness of Secular Work — Jordan Raynor

Introduction: Why This Book

Reflection Questions
  • Do you ever feel guilty that your "secular" work isn't as important as ministry work?
  • What aspects of your job feel most disconnected from your faith?
  • How would it change your work if you believed it had eternal value?
Action Step: Take one area of work you've dismissed and pray: "Lord, show me how this matters eternally."

Way #1: God's Pleasure in Your Work

Reflection Questions
  • What brings you the most joy in your work? Does it align with what brings God joy?
  • When do you rush through tasks? What might you miss by hurrying?
  • How can you work with delight, as an act of worship?
Action Step: Choose one task you usually rush—tomorrow, do it slowly, with delight and gratitude, as worship.

Way #3: Pulling Heaven into Earth

Reflection Questions
  • What would a "kingdom habit" look like in your workplace?
  • Where can you bring beauty, justice, kindness, or rest into your work environment?
  • How does your work participate in God's renewal of all things?
Action Step: Add one small kingdom habit today (kindness, rest, justice) into your work setting intentionally.

Legacy: Work That Outlasts You

Reflection Questions
  • What do you want to be remembered for in your work?
  • How might your current faithfulness echo into eternity?
  • What eternal outcome do you hope God uses your work to accomplish?
Action Step: Write a 50-word statement of the eternal outcome you hope God uses your work for—even after you're gone.

Memory Verses

"God is not unjust so as to overlook your work and the love that you have shown for his name." — Hebrews 6:10 (ESV)

"Let your light shine before others, so that they may see your good works and give glory to your Father who is in heaven." — Matthew 5:16 (ESV)

Daily Workflow Integration

Use this simple rhythm to live out what you're learning:

Morning

At the top of your day, pick one "truth" from your reading to carry. Write it on a card or set it as a phone reminder.

Midday

Pause and ask: "Is what I'm doing reflecting that truth?" Adjust course if needed.

Close of Day

Journal one way you lived it—or confess where you drifted. Thank God for grace and commit to try again tomorrow.

Monthly Check-In

Pause once a month to evaluate your growth:

  • What have I seen changed in my work posture or heart?
  • Which action steps did I follow? Which felt lifeless? Why?
  • What patterns of sin or growth am I noticing?
  • What's one next step God is calling me to?
  • What book should I read next?
