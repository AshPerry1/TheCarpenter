Small Group Study Guide

The Theology of Work: A 6-Week Journey

How to Use This Guide

This 6-week study helps your small group explore the biblical theology of work. Each session includes Scripture reading, discussion questions, and practical application. Encourage honest conversation and support one another in seeing your everyday work as worship.

Week 1: Work as God's Gift

Before the Fall, God gave work

"Then God said, 'Let us make man in our image, after our likeness. And let them have dominion...' The LORD God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to work it and keep it." — Genesis 1:26–28; 2:15

Discussion Questions

How does understanding that God gave work before the Fall change your view of your job?
What are some ways your work allows you to "exercise dominion" or create value in the world?
When do you most feel that your work is meaningful? When does it feel meaningless?
How can we help each other see our jobs as gifts from God rather than necessary evils?
This Week's Practice: Each morning, thank God for one specific aspect of your job.

Week 2: Work as Worship

Doing all things "as for the Lord"

"Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men, knowing that from the Lord you will receive the inheritance as your reward. You are serving the Lord Christ." — Colossians 3:23–24

Discussion Questions

What would change in your work if you truly believed Jesus was your direct manager?
In what areas of your job are you tempted to work for human approval rather than God's glory?
How can mundane tasks (emails, meetings, etc.) become acts of worship?
Share one way you'll practice working "as for the Lord" this week.
This Week's Practice: Before each task, pause and dedicate it to the Lord.

Week 3: Work with Integrity

Truth-telling in all things

"The integrity of the upright guides them, but the crookedness of the treacherous destroys them." — Proverbs 11:3

Discussion Questions

Where are you most tempted to compromise integrity at work? (Time, expenses, communication, etc.)
What does it cost to maintain integrity in your workplace?
How does our integrity (or lack of it) affect our witness to coworkers?
How can we hold each other accountable to work with integrity?
This Week's Practice: Report time and expenses with complete accuracy. Confess and correct any areas of compromise.

Week 4: Work as Service

Loving your neighbor through your vocation

"For you were called to freedom, brothers. Only do not use your freedom as an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another." — Galatians 5:13

Discussion Questions

Who does your work serve? (Customers, coworkers, community, etc.)
How does your job meet real human needs?
In what ways can you better serve others through your work this week?
How does seeing work as service change your motivation?
This Week's Practice: Each day, identify one person your work serves and pray for them.

Week 5: Eternal Significance

Your labor is not in vain

"Therefore, my beloved brothers, be steadfast, immovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that in the Lord your labor is not in vain." — 1 Corinthians 15:58

Discussion Questions

What parts of your work feel most meaningless? How might they have eternal significance?
How does knowing God sees and remembers your work change your perspective?
In what ways might your current work echo into eternity?
How can we encourage each other when work feels futile?
This Week's Practice: When tasks feel meaningless, remind yourself: "In the Lord, this is not in vain."

Week 6: Work as Witness

Letting your light shine

"In the same way, let your light shine before others, so that they may see your good works and give glory to your Father who is in heaven." — Matthew 5:16

Discussion Questions

How does your work provide opportunities to point others to Christ?
What kind of reputation do you have at work? Does it adorn the gospel?
How can we be bold witnesses without being obnoxious?
Who in your workplace needs to hear about Jesus? How will you pray for them?
This Week's Practice: Pray daily for one coworker's salvation. Be ready to share the gospel if God opens a door.
